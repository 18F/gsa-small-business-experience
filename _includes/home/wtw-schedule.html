Sell directly to one of the largest buyers of services and goods in the world, the U.S. Federal government. GSA schedules are long-term contracts that allow federal agencies to buy products and services directly from businesses. Schedule purchases amount to approximately $36 Billion per year.

Getting on a GSA schedule

Hold a Schedule contract can open doors for your business, but requires time and resources to be succesful. It can take 6-12 months from the time you submit your offer.

Before submitting an offer you'll need to:
  • Register with SAM.gov
  • Certify with Small Business Administration (SBA) for any set-asides
  • Understand the soliciation
  • Complete self-led PDF trainings: Pathways to success and Readiness Assessment

Assemble & submit offer

Once you're ready, you will assemble all required documents and submit your business proposal to get on GSA's schedule.

Review & negotiate

Your offer will be reviewed and you will be able to review and negotiate changes to the contract schedule.

3
Sell & maintain

Once you have been approved and are on the GSA schedule you will be able to begin marketing and selling directly to federal agencies.
